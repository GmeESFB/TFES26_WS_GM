🎯 Two Layers of Prompt Precision

▶ Layer 1 — Form (prefixes)

Prefixes and modifiers tell the AI how to structure its response: length, tone, reasoning depth, output format. They are signals about the shape of the answer.

L99 BEASTMODE /noyap

🌿 Layer 2 — Substance (vocabulary)

Precise words embedded inside your prompt activate specific knowledge architectures in the AI. A single exact term can replace three sentences of explanation — and unlock a much richer response.

scaffold  •  elicit  •  steelman

🌿 Semantic Seeds — High-Value Vocabulary for Teachers
These words, used inside your prompt content (not as prefixes), activate richer, more pedagogically precise AI responses. They are the difference between a generic answer and one that matches your actual teaching intent.
Word What it activates in the AI Example use in a prompt
scaffold AI builds a structured support (partial worked example, hint sequence, sentence frames) before the full independent task — not just a simpler version. "Create a scaffolded writing task for B1 learners on the causes of WW1."
elicit AI designs prompts, questions, or activities that draw out student thinking rather than deliver content. Shifts from transmission to inquiry. "Write 5 questions to elicit prior knowledge about fractions before the lesson."
misconception AI targets a specific, named student error and builds an activity around its root cause — not a general review, but a surgical correction. "Design an exercise that addresses the common misconception that plants get their mass from soil."
probe AI generates follow-up questions designed to deepen or test a student's reasoning — Socratic, diagnostic, going beyond the surface answer. "Add 3 probe questions for each correct answer to push Expert-pathway students further."
steelman AI presents the strongest possible version of an argument before critiquing it. Teaches intellectual honesty and avoids strawmanning in debate exercises. "Steelman the argument that social media has been positive for democracy, then list its weaknesses."
operationalise AI turns a vague learning intention into a concrete, measurable classroom activity or assessment criterion. Forces specificity. "Operationalise the competency 'critical thinking' into 3 observable student behaviours for S4."
extrapolate AI extends a principle, rule, or finding into an unfamiliar context — exactly what Expert-pathway tasks require at Bloom's Evaluate level. "Ask students to extrapolate Newton's second law to explain the motion of a satellite."
calibrate AI adjusts vocabulary, sentence length, and cognitive demand to a precise target level — more granular than simply saying "make it easier." "Calibrate this text to CEFR A2 while preserving all historical facts."
Gestalt AI considers the whole conceptual pattern first, then the parts — useful for synthesis tasks and for helping students see the "big picture" before details. "Give me the Gestalt of the French Revolution before we break it into causes and phases."
subsume AI shows how a new concept fits within a broader category the student already knows — activates Ausubel's meaningful learning and concept mapping. "Explain how ionic bonding subsumes the idea of electron transfer students met in primary science."
🌿 Combine seeds with prefixes: BEASTMODE /noyap Create an activity that probes the misconception that heavier objects fall faster, calibrated to S3.
