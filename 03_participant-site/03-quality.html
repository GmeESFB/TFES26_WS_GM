Three-Tier Quality Assurance v2

Self-review → Peer validation → Student feedback
Self-Review Checklist
Complete before sharing with peers or using in class. Use alongside the Hallucination Detection Grid.
Factual check passed — Hallucination Grid Zone 1 = 100%
Syllabus alignment confirmed — content matches official programme
All 4 pathways tested — tried exercises as if you were a student in each pathway
Bloom's levels verified — Discovery ≠ just easier; Autonomy ≠ just harder
Instructions standalone — a student can follow without extra verbal explanation
Timing estimated — activities fit within realistic class time
Answer key correct — all expected answers verified independently
Inclusive language — no stereotypes, patronising tone, or cultural assumptions
Peer Validation Protocol
Ask a colleague (ideally from the same or adjacent discipline) to review using this framework.

Ask your peer reviewer:

Content accuracy — "Do you spot any factual errors I might have missed?"
Difficulty calibration — "Does each pathway feel right for its target group?"
Clarity — "Can you follow the Discovery instructions without asking me questions?"
Autonomy pathway — "Does the S6-S7 pathway genuinely promote independence?"
Bias check — "Do any examples feel culturally or socially exclusive?"
Overall verdict — "Would you use this with your students as-is, or what would you change?"
🎯 Structured Peer Prompt
You can also ask an AI to act as a peer reviewer:
Act as a critical colleague reviewing my AI-generated classroom materials. Check: (1) factual accuracy for the subject and cycle, (2) difficulty calibration per pathway, (3) syllabus alignment, (4) any implicit bias or cultural exclusion. For each issue found: quote the problem, explain why it is a problem, and suggest a fix. End with an overall recommendation: ready to use / minor edits needed / major revision required.
Student Feedback Protocol
Collect structured feedback after the first classroom use to improve your materials iteratively.

Quick 3-question exit survey (2 min):

Q1 (Clarity): "The instructions were clear enough for me to work independently." (1-5 scale)
Q2 (Fit): "The difficulty level felt right for me." (Too easy / Just right / Too hard)
Q3 (Open): "One thing I would change about this activity is…" (free text)
Iteration Checklist
After collecting student feedback, use this checklist before the next use.
Discovery pathway: were students able to work independently? If not, add more scaffolding.
Standard pathway: did it cover the syllabus without being too easy or too hard?
Expert pathway: did it genuinely challenge advanced students or did they finish too quickly?
Autonomy pathway: did S6-S7 students use the learning contract actively?
Timing: did activities fit the session? Adjust estimates for next time.
Student voice: was the Q3 feedback actionable? Document changes made.
